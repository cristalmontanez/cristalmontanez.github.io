In Memory of My Journey About Me

Transition to Project Leadership (2012 - 2016)

In 2012, I transitioned to Global Resources as a team lead, focusing on client relations, coordinating development plans, and gathering customer requirements. This role deepened my understanding of project management and strengthened my relationship-building skills with key client stakeholders. In 2013, my career took a new turn when I joined CoolTribes as a Scrum Master. For three years, I embraced agile methodology, managing sprints, retrospectives, and project planning to drive customer-focused outcomes. I encouraged a learning-focused environment where my team could deliver quality results efficiently. Working in this agile space taught me how to guide teams toward delivering impactful products quickly and effectively, while also allowing me to cultivate strong organizational and coaching skills.

Early Beginnings in Technology (2007 - 2011)

I was born in a small town in Venezuela, where I pursued my passion for technology and earned a bachelor’s degree in computer science, graduating in 2007. My first job in technology was as a developer at DB ACCESS, where I worked from 2007 to 2011. I began as part of the development team, building and enhancing systems for banking clients. Using tools like ASP.NET, C#, and SQL Server, I contributed to a variety of projects, including risk management and electronic banking systems. Later, I moved into my first leadership role, where I planned and supervised complex development tasks. Working in this environment taught me the importance of attention to detail, clear communication, and collaborative problem-solving—skills that laid the foundation for everything that followed.

Moving to the United States and Entering Healthcare Interpretation (2016 - 2021)

In 2016, I moved to the United States to improve my English. By 2018, I began my journey in healthcare interpretation, joining the Asian Association of Utah. Providing interpretation services to connect patients with medical staff was deeply rewarding. From 2019 to 2021, I worked as a PRN interpreter at the Huntsman Cancer Institute, gaining valuable experience by assisting patients in an emotionally sensitive field. I balanced my time between in-person, phone, and video appointments, always maintaining strict confidentiality and professionalism. Each encounter reaffirmed my commitment to compassionate, clear communication.

Personal Challenges and New Perspectives (2020)

In 2020, I was diagnosed with breast cancer—a battle that profoundly changed my perspective on life. It shifted my priorities, deepening my appreciation for my family and my relationship with God. Even though my diagnosis was difficult, I am grateful for God’s mercy in giving me a second chance at life.

Advancing in Medical Interpretation at University Hospital (2023 - Present)

In February 2023, I joined University Hospital as a medical interpreter, providing verbal interpretation and coordinating patient-staff communications across various settings—whether over the phone, in person, or via video. I prepared records, assisted patients with forms, and adhered strictly to HIPAA laws, which required the utmost discretion and ethical conduct. Most recently, in June 2023, I was promoted to a leadership role at University Hospital. Now, I lead a diverse team of interpreters proficient in Spanish, Arabic, Somali, and Dari. My responsibilities include managing their schedules, overseeing training, and ensuring our services meet the clinic’s unique needs. Working closely with administration, I strive to create an empowering environment for my team, one where patient care and language services align seamlessly. Today, I am proud to bring together my background in technology, agile leadership, and medical interpretation. From developing software to bridging language gaps, my career has always been driven by a dedication to fostering understanding, efficiency, and compassion in every role I undertake.
