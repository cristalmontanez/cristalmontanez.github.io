In Memory of My Journey About Me

Transition to Project Leadership (2012 - 2016)

In 2012, I transitioned to Global Resources as a team lead, focusing on client relations, coordinating development plans, and gathering customer requirements. This role deepened my understanding of project management and strengthened my relationship-building skills with key client stakeholders. In 2013, my career took a new turn when I joined CoolTribes as a Scrum Master. For three years, I embraced agile methodology, managing sprints, retrospectives, and project planning to drive customer-focused outcomes. Working in this agile space taught me how to guide teams toward delivering impactful products quickly and effectively, while also allowing me to cultivate strong organizational and coaching skills.

Early Beginnings in Technology (2007 - 2011)

I was born in a small town in Venezuela, where I pursued my passion for technology and earned a bachelor’s degree in computer science, graduating in 2007. My first job in technology was as a developer at DB ACCESS, where I worked from 2007 to 2011. Using tools like ASP.NET, C#, and SQL Server, I contributed to various projects, including risk management and electronic banking systems.

Moving to the United States and Entering Healthcare Interpretation (2016 - 2021)

In 2016, I moved to the United States to improve my English. By 2018, I began my journey in healthcare interpretation, joining the Asian Association of Utah.

Personal Challenges and New Perspectives (2020)

In 2020, I was diagnosed with breast cancer—a battle that profoundly changed my perspective on life. It shifted my priorities, deepening my appreciation for my family and my relationship with God.

Advancing in Medical Interpretation at University Hospital (2023 - Present)

In February 2023, I joined University Hospital as a medical interpreter. In June 2023, I was promoted to a leadership role, managing a diverse team of interpreters.
